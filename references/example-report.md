## ⚔️ Devil's Advocate

**Burry says:** DeFi TVL is circular and self-referential. Much of Aave's TVL is recursive leverage (deposit ETH → borrow USDC → buy more ETH → deposit again). In a real deleveraging event, TVL collapses 70% in days, and the "revenue" disappears with it.

**Graham says:** There's no liquidation value. If Aave protocol stops, token holders get nothing — no assets, no cash, no book value. At $2.8B FDV, you're paying entirely for future cash flows with zero margin of safety. The governance token has no legal claim on protocol revenue.

**Druckenmiller says:** DeFi correlates with ETH, which correlates with BTC, which correlates with global liquidity. If the Fed tightens again or a credit event hits, this is a levered bet on risk-on. You're not buying a lending business — you're buying a liquidity derivative.

**Strongest bear case:** Token holders have no enforceable claim on protocol revenue. Revenue flows to a DAO treasury, and governance can redirect it at any time. This is fundamentally different from equity ownership.

**My response:** Valid concern. The mitigation: Aave governance has consistently voted for buyback-and-distribute mechanisms, and the Safety Module aligns staker incentives with protocol health. The lack of legal claim is a DeFi-wide issue, not Aave-specific — if you're investing in DeFi at all, you've accepted this risk. I'd exit if governance proposals shift to redirecting revenue away from token holders.
