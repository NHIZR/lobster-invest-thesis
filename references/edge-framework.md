# Edge Framework

Your Edge is the systematic advantage you bring to investment decisions. Without one, you're gambling with extra steps.

---

## What Is an Edge?

An edge is a repeatable pattern where your analysis produces better outcomes than the market's default pricing. It's not luck. It's not "I read more." It's a specific type of mispricing you've learned to identify.

## How to Define Yours

Answer these three questions for each potential edge:

1. **What pattern do you see that others consistently miss?**
   Be specific. "I'm good at research" is not an edge. "I notice when market sentiment on geopolitical events overshoots the actual economic impact" is an edge.

2. **Why do you see it when others don't?**
   Professional background, geographic perspective, domain expertise, information sources, analytical framework. Something concrete.

3. **When has this worked before?**
   Name 2-3 past trades or decisions where this edge produced alpha. If you can't name any, it's a hypothesis, not an edge. (Hypotheses are fine — just label them as such.)

## Edge Template

Add to `config.json` → `edge_definitions`:

```json
{
  "name": "Short descriptive name",
  "description": "2-3 sentences explaining the pattern you exploit",
  "trigger": "Specific conditions that activate this edge",
  "track_record": "Brief note on past results (optional but valuable)"
}
```

## Common Edge Types

### Information Edge
You access data others don't (or don't use). Examples:
- On-chain analysis revealing accumulation before it shows in price
- Industry contacts providing channel checks
- Monitoring obscure but leading indicators

### Analytical Edge
You process the same information differently. Examples:
- Second-order thinking: event → who benefits → what they'll do → supply/demand shift
- Cross-market pattern recognition (DeFi yields predicting crypto rotation)
- Faster mental models for complex scenarios

### Behavioral Edge
You exploit systematic emotional biases. Examples:
- Buying geopolitical panic when historical mean reversion is >80%
- Selling into hype cycles at specific sentiment indicators
- Patience to hold through volatility that shakes out weak hands

### Temporal Edge
You have a different time horizon than the marginal buyer. Examples:
- 3-year thesis on a stock that funds evaluate quarterly
- Willingness to lock up capital in illiquid opportunities
- Catalyst timing — knowing when the market will be forced to re-price

## Using Your Edge in Reports

Every report starts with Step 0: Edge Check.

```
Edge match: [Name of edge] — [1-sentence explanation of why it applies]
```

or

```
Edge match: None — Proceeding with standard analysis. Conclusion defaults to "Watch."
```

Reports with a matching edge get higher conviction. Reports without one aren't worthless — but the bar for "Go" is higher because you're competing without an advantage.

---

## Evolving Your Edge

Review quarterly:
- Which edges produced alpha? Keep and refine.
- Which edges looked good in theory but failed in practice? Demote to hypothesis.
- What new patterns are you noticing? Promote to edge candidate.

Track this in your report archive. The pattern becomes visible over time.
