# Devil's Advocate Execution Guide

**Required in every report.** No exceptions.

The process: write your thesis, then switch sides and attack it through three investment philosophies. If you can't articulate the strongest counter-argument, your thesis isn't ready.

---

## The Three Attackers

### 1. Michael Burry (Contrarian)

**Mindset:** The crowd is almost always wrong at extremes. What are they missing?

**Attack vectors:**
- What consensus assumption is priced in that could break?
- Is there a structural risk nobody's discussing because it's uncomfortable?
- What happened last time this narrative was this popular?
- Is there hidden leverage or fragility in the system?

**Burry's favorite weapons:**
- Accounting irregularities
- Unsustainable unit economics masked by growth
- Bubble dynamics: "everyone knows" = nobody's thinking
- Regulatory risk that's been ignored because it's inconvenient

### 2. Benjamin Graham (Safety Margin)

**Mindset:** What's the downside? How much do I lose if I'm wrong?

**Attack vectors:**
- Liquidation value: if the business stops tomorrow, what do shareholders get?
- Is management buying or selling shares?
- How much of the valuation depends on future growth vs. current assets?
- What's the worst-case scenario, and can I survive it?

**Graham's favorite weapons:**
- Price-to-book below 1.0 or above 5.0 (extremes)
- Management compensation vs. shareholder returns
- Debt maturity schedule
- Dividend history (cut = red flag)

### 3. Stanley Druckenmiller (Macro Cycle)

**Mindset:** Fundamentals matter, but liquidity and positioning matter more in the short term.

**Attack vectors:**
- Is the macro environment supporting or fighting this thesis?
- If interest rates move 200bp in either direction, what happens?
- How crowded is this trade? What happens when positions unwind?
- Is this a "first to break" or "last to break" asset in a downturn?

**Druckenmiller's favorite weapons:**
- Central bank policy shifts
- Credit cycle positioning
- Currency cross-currents
- Consensus positioning data (COT, fund flows)

---

## Execution Steps

1. **Write your thesis first.** Full conviction. Don't hedge preemptively.

2. **Switch roles.** For each attacker, spend 2-3 minutes asking: "Why would [Burry/Graham/Druckenmiller] short this or pass?"

3. **Find the strongest single counter-argument.** Synthesize all three attacks into the one thing most likely to break your thesis.

4. **Write your response.** Either:
   - Explain why the counter-argument doesn't hold (with evidence)
   - Acknowledge it and define the condition under which you'd surrender

5. **Format the output:**

```markdown
## ⚔️ Devil's Advocate

**Burry says:** [1-2 sentences, the sharpest contrarian objection]
**Graham says:** [1-2 sentences, the safety margin concern]
**Druckenmiller says:** [1-2 sentences, the macro/cycle risk]

**Strongest bear case:** [Synthesis — the single most dangerous threat]
**My response:** [Why you still hold / what would make you capitulate]
```

---

## Quality Check

A weak Devil's Advocate section is worse than none. Signs of weakness:
- Straw-man objections that are easy to dismiss
- All three attackers saying variations of the same thing
- "My response" that doesn't engage with the actual objection
- Missing specific numbers or evidence in the response

A strong section makes you uncomfortable. If it doesn't, you're not trying hard enough.
