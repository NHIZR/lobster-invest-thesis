# The Six Investment Lenses

Pick 2-3 per target. Using all six means you haven't figured out what matters.

---

## 1. Quality Compounder (Buffett)

**Question:** Will this business look stronger in 10 years?

**What to measure:**
- ROIC trend over 5+ years (rising = good)
- Moat type: network effects, switching costs, brand, scale, regulatory capture
- Pricing power: can they raise prices without losing customers?
- Capital allocation: management returns cash or reinvests at high ROIC?
- Insider ownership: does management eat their own cooking?

**When to use:** Established companies with track records. Not for early-stage bets.

**Kill signal:** ROIC declining for 3+ consecutive quarters. New competitor eroding moat.

---

## 2. Imaginative Growth (a16z)

**Question:** What's the maximum plausible outcome if everything goes right?

**What to measure:**
- TAM: current vs. addressable in 5 years (real TAM, not slide-deck TAM)
- Revenue growth rate: >40% YoY for software, >100% for crypto
- User/developer adoption curve: hockey stick or linear?
- Unit economics: improving or subsidized?
- Network effects: does each new user make the product better?

**When to use:** Disruptors, new categories, zero-to-one products.

**Kill signal:** Growth decelerating two quarters in a row. Unit economics worsening at scale.

---

## 3. Fundamental Long/Short (Tiger Cubs)

**Question:** What does deep diligence reveal that the market misses?

**What to measure:**
- Variant perception: your view vs. consensus (and why you're right)
- EV/EBITDA, P/E, P/S relative to growth and quality
- Management quality: track record, incentive alignment, capital allocation history
- Channel checks: what do customers, suppliers, competitors say?
- Short interest: crowded short = squeeze risk; low short + overvalued = opportunity

**When to use:** Assets where the market has a specific, identifiable blind spot.

**Kill signal:** Your variant thesis gets priced in (stock moves to fair value). New information invalidates your edge.

---

## 4. Deep Value (Klarman)

**Question:** What would a private buyer pay for this entire business today?

**What to measure:**
- Margin of safety: how far below intrinsic value?
- Replacement cost: what does it cost to build this from scratch?
- Balance sheet: net cash, hidden assets, off-balance-sheet liabilities
- Free cash flow yield: >8% = interesting, >12% = compelling
- Catalyst: what forces the market to recognize the value?

**When to use:** Beaten-down, hated assets where the price implies permanent impairment that won't happen.

**Kill signal:** Fundamental deterioration that justifies the low price. Value trap: cheap for a reason.

---

## 5. Event-Driven (Ackman)

**Question:** What specific event will force a re-pricing?

**What to measure:**
- Catalyst type: earnings surprise, spin-off, activist involvement, regulatory change, M&A
- Timeline: when does the event happen? (Vague catalysts = no edge)
- Magnitude: how much will the price move if the event occurs?
- Probability: realistic odds, not hope
- Downside if event doesn't happen: do you still own something decent?

**When to use:** Special situations where timing is identifiable and magnitude is large.

**Kill signal:** Catalyst timeline passes without event. Setup changes fundamentally.

---

## 6. Macro-Tactical (Druckenmiller)

**Question:** Where are we in the cycle, and what does that mean for this asset?

**What to measure:**
- Interest rate trajectory: tightening or easing?
- Liquidity: M2 growth, Fed balance sheet, credit conditions
- Cycle position: early expansion, late cycle, recession, recovery
- Currency and commodity headwinds/tailwinds
- Positioning: is everyone already on this trade?

**When to use:** Timing decisions, risk-on vs. risk-off calls, sector rotation.

**Kill signal:** Macro regime change that invalidates the thesis. Central bank pivot.

---

## Combining Lenses

Strong reports typically combine:
- One **fundamental** lens (1, 3, or 4) for valuation grounding
- One **catalyst** lens (2, 5, or 6) for timing and upside

Example combinations:
- Quality Compounder + Event-Driven = Great business at a temporary discount due to a specific event
- Imaginative Growth + Macro-Tactical = Disruptor positioned for the right part of the cycle
- Deep Value + Fundamental L/S = Market blind spot on an undervalued asset
